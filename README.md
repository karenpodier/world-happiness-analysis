# World Happiness Analysis

## Project Overview
This project explores the World Happiness dataset to analyse the key factors that influence happiness across countries. The analysis focuses on relationships between happiness scores and variables such as GDP, social support, health, freedom, corruption, and generosity.

## Objectives
- Analyse global happiness rankings
- Identify key drivers of happiness
- Compare top and bottom performing countries
- Explore relationships between different wellbeing factors

## Tools Used
- SQL (PostgreSQL)
- PowerPoint (data visualisation)

## Files Included
- `world_happiness_analysis.pdf` – Full analysis presentation

## Key Insights
- Economic wealth alone does not determine happiness
- Social support and health are strong drivers of wellbeing
- Lower corruption is associated with higher happiness
- Generosity has a weaker relationship with happiness compared to other factors

## Conclusion
This analysis highlights that wellbeing is influenced by multiple social and institutional factors, and not just economic performance. Policymakers should focus on improving quality of life through healthcare, social support, and governance.
